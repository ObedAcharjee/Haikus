Haikus

Haiku is a type of short-form poetry that originated in Japan. Traditional Japanese haiku consists of three phrases composed of 17 syllables in a 5, 7, 5 pattern. There is more to Haikus than just adhering to the syllable count, However I have decided to take a different approach and base mine on feelings, emotions, or observations.

War

I wake up distraught
Death and Despair surrounds me
Peace seems out of reach

Ayaka

Serene and Graceful
Is how i would describe her
An ethereal sight

Regrets

If only i could
Turn back time and push myself
To make her just mine

Lone

A half of both Worlds
Bearing the pain of both sides
His life had been hell

Selfless

He marches forward
At the expense of his life
All for his people

Dazed

Beneath the Night Sky
Wandering around the fields
She left me enthralled.

Twilight Bonds

Evening strolls with her
Jokes and Gossip, abundant
Memories I cherish.

Control

Your days are numbered
She whispers softly to me
No hint of malice

Frieren

Curse or a Blessing
this life of mine, ample time
but lone i wander...

Contrast

Conflicting views
Polar opposites and yet
still together

Joyboy

Times changed, so did friends
But you always stayed the same
So bright and cheerful

Evolve

Will you stay the same?
Or go beyond your limits
And alter your fate.

All haikus written by yours truly, L.B.
